# SCOUTS Visual Design Deep Dive

**Based on:** The SCOUTS (choices) script, 14 Fordham production photos (dir. Juju Jaworski, October 2024), and the Juilliard reading reference.

---

## What the Production Photos Reveal

The Fordham production is the reference. Everything that follows is informed by what's actually on that stage.

### Costume Design

The uniforms are **real BSA uniforms** -- tan/khaki short-sleeve button-up shirts, cargo shorts or pants, and importantly: **Troop 5701** patch visible on the sleeve. American flag patch on the right shoulder. Each character wears the uniform differently, and that difference IS characterization.

**Critical discovery:** The costumes CHANGE throughout the show. The scout uniform is the formal mask. As the play progresses and masks come off, so do layers of clothing. By the fight scene, shirts are removed -- boys are in tank tops and undershirts. By Brent's final monologue, he's in a blood-spattered white wifebeater. During the Gay Shit scenes, Simon changes into a PINK STRIPED SHIRT -- not the uniform at all. Simon's true self emerges through costume.

### Lighting Design

The lighting is the emotional language of the show:
- **Cool blue/purple** = The Gay Shit scenes. Safety. Intimacy. Sam and Simon bathed in blue.
- **Harsh red wash** = The fight. The ceremony. Violence and aggression. Brent's world.
- **Warm green-gold spotlight** = Monologues. Vulnerability. Single figure illuminated, everything else black.
- **Cold neutral** = The opening, the formal scenes. No comfort.

This must be reflected in the game -- not just as background colors, but as actual tinting of the ENTIRE scene including sprites.

### Staging & Physicality

- Brent stands SEPARATE from the group. Often stage right, holding the cooler, elevated. He paces.
- The boys stand in a LINE during formal scenes -- military formation, arms at 90 degrees.
- Simon and Sam gravitate toward each other even in group scenes.
- During the Gay Shit, Simon and Sam sit BACK-TO-BACK, then progress to head-in-lap.
- During the fight, bodies are low to the ground, tangled, chaotic.
- Monologues: single figure in a circle of light, everyone else in darkness or collapsed on the edges.

### Set Design

- **Branch arch:** An arch of sticks/branches upstage, like a forest gateway or a cage.
- **Tire:** A tire on the ground, center-ish, where the "campfire" is (yellow/green ribbons inside -- the campfire that never gets lit because Brent lied about s'mores).
- **White cooler:** Brent's prop. Contains the squirrels, the sex doll. Symbol of his authority.
- **Wooden blocks/crates:** Used as seating, platforms, props.
- **Rope:** Hanging from the arch, suggesting restraint, wilderness.
- **Earth-toned floor:** Dirt, leaves.

## Character Visual Design (Comprehensive)

### SIMON (Player Character)

**From the script:** 12 years old. "The faggot." Likes dolphins, mermaids, butterflies. Carries a tote bag. Walks "like a fag." A strawberry, not a tomato. Trans-coded -- "maybe I think I'm a girl."

**From the photos:** Curly brown hair (the curliest in the cast). Smaller frame. In the Juilliard reading, wearing a butterfly cardigan and jean skirt -- that's who Simon WANTS to be. In the production, wearing the scout uniform loosely, almost like it doesn't fit right. Pink/red striped shirt underneath visible at the collar. During the Gay Shit: the scout shirt comes off, the pink shirt is who they are.

**Sprite design (48x64):**
- **Hair:** Curly, brown, visible volume -- 2-3 pixels wider than other characters' hair. Slightly longer, falls past the ears. This is the most distinguishing silhouette feature.
- **Build:** Smallest of the group. Narrow shoulders (12px wide). The sprite should look like the uniform is slightly too big.
- **Scout shirt:** Tan/khaki, but UNTUCKED and slightly open at the collar, revealing a pink/lavender undershirt (2-3 pixels of color at the neckline). Sleeves look a bit long.
- **Neckerchief:** Loose, not properly tied. The knot is off-center.
- **Shorts:** Khaki cargo shorts, slightly too long (past the knee on Simon, above the knee on everyone else).
- **Shoes:** Converse-style sneakers. Lavender/purple. This is the one non-regulation item. 2px of color at the feet that reads as "this person is different."
- **Posture:** Weight on one hip. Not standing straight. Slightly turned, like they're ready to leave.
- **Merit badges:** NONE visible. Simon isn't invested in scouting.
- **Special detail:** A 1px tote bag strap over one shoulder. Just a line, but it's there.

**Idle animation:** Shifts weight side to side. Occasionally looks down at their feet. Touches their arm (self-comfort gesture = slight arm pixel shift).

**Gay Shit costume change:** When entering Cliff/Meadow/Lake scenes, Simon's sprite swaps to: pink striped shirt, no scout uniform, hair looks softer/freer. This is a VISIBLE transformation. The player sees Simon become more themselves.

### SAM

**From the script:** 12. The "straight" one. Sus. Discreet. Suave, genuinely. Endearing. Guilty. Notoriously difficult to read. Prays before bed. Has a crush on Simon. The pillow. The Jesus boner.

**From the photos:** Darker skin, short dark hair, well-groomed. Uniform is properly worn -- buttoned, tucked, neat. He looks like the "good kid." In the intimate scenes he leans IN toward Simon, close, protective. His body language is careful but magnetic.

**Sprite design (48x64):**
- **Hair:** Short, dark (near-black), neat. Clean line. The opposite of Simon's chaos.
- **Build:** Average. Well-proportioned. 14px shoulders. Stands straight but not stiff.
- **Scout shirt:** Properly buttoned and tucked. The "correct" way to wear it. This is Sam performing straightness -- he follows the rules because breaking them is terrifying.
- **Neckerchief:** Properly tied with a slide (the small clasp). The red/maroon color should be visible (3-4 pixels).
- **Shorts:** Standard length, above the knee. Dark olive/brown.
- **Shoes:** Regular boots/shoes. Brown. Nothing stands out. Sam doesn't draw attention to himself.
- **Posture:** Upright, hands at sides or clasped in front. Controlled. But he's always ANGLED slightly toward wherever Simon is.
- **Merit badges:** A few -- maybe 2-3 small colored squares on the left pocket area. Sam actually tries at scouting because his parents put him here.
- **Special detail:** A small cross necklace. 1-2 pixels of gold at the neckline. This is his faith, his guilt, his prayer.

**Idle animation:** Still, composed. Occasionally looks to the side (toward Simon's position). Slight forward lean when Simon is speaking.

**Gay Shit costume change:** Sam's scout shirt opens/unbuttons (collar looser, more relaxed). He doesn't fully change -- he can't let go of the structure the way Simon can. But he softens.

### BRENT

**From the script:** 16. Alpha. Top dog. "About to be an eagle scout" but hasn't taken steps. Held back "for sports." His dad is gay and left. His mom calls him a faggot. He doesn't cry. He likes flowers and butterflies and kindness but he can't be those things.

**From the photos:** GLASSES. This is huge -- the alpha male wears glasses. He's bigger than everyone (he's 16, they're 11-12). Carries the white cooler everywhere like a weapon. In the opening, he's separated from the group, pacing. During his final monologue: blood-soaked white tank top, dog tags, hands on his head, screaming. The scout shirt is GONE -- his armor is stripped.

**Sprite design (48x64):**
- **Hair:** Buzzcut. Almost military-short. Dark. 2px of hair, barely there.
- **Build:** THE BIGGEST. 20px wide shoulders. Uses the full 64px height. Everything about this sprite says "I take up space." Thick neck.
- **Scout shirt:** Worn like a drill sergeant's uniform. Tucked tight, sleeves rolled up to show forearms (thicker arms than anyone else). But here's the detail: his shirt has MORE patches than anyone. He's overcompensating.
- **Neckerchief:** Red, prominently displayed. The slide is polished. This is his badge of authority.
- **Pants:** Full-length cargo pants (not shorts). He's the only one in pants. Because he's 16 and he's a "man."
- **Shoes:** Heavy boots. Dark. Wide. You can hear them.
- **Posture:** Wide stance. Chest out. Arms slightly away from body (takes up maximum width). Head slightly forward -- predatory lean.
- **Glasses:** 2x4 pixel rectangle across the eyes. This is the humanizing detail. The alpha wears glasses. He can't see without them.
- **Special detail:** The cooler. When Brent is on screen, the white cooler should be visible near his position (a separate small sprite, 16x16, white/blue). It contains the squirrels, the sex doll, the instruments of his authority.
- **Merit badges:** The most of anyone -- his sash or shirt is covered. He's performing Eagle Scout even though he hasn't earned it.
- **Troop number:** "5701" on the sleeve (even at pixel scale, a dark patch with lighter pixels suggesting numbers).

**Idle animation:** PACING. Not standing still. His idle is a 2-3 pixel back-and-forth movement. Occasionally punches his own palm (fist into hand). When he's not speaking, he's surveilling -- his head turns.

**Fight scene costume change:** Scout shirt ripped off. White tank top. Blood appears as red pixels progressively added to the sprite during the fight sequence. By his final monologue: the sprite is tank top + blood + no glasses (they got knocked off). He looks smaller without the uniform. He IS smaller. He's 16 but he's a kid.

### JOSH

**From the script:** 11 and three quarters. The brawn. Future Marine. World War One affinity. Beta-like tendencies. His dad takes out his belt. "SON, YOU'RE GOING TO BE A MARINE." He wanted to make pizza.

**From the photos:** Stocky build, short hair. Eager body language -- he's always leaning forward, toward Brent, trying to be noticed. He's the first to volunteer. He's the first to follow orders.

**Sprite design (48x64):**
- **Hair:** Short, military-adjacent cut. Brown. Parted to one side. Clean but not as precise as Sam.
- **Build:** STOCKY. Not as tall as Brent but WIDE for his age. 18px shoulders on a 58px-tall frame. He's been going to the gym with his dad. His arms are slightly bigger than the other 12-year-olds.
- **Scout shirt:** PERFECT regulation. Tucked, buttoned, crisp. But where Sam wears it neatly because he's careful, Josh wears it neatly because his dad MAKES him. There's a tension in how straight it sits.
- **Neckerchief:** Regulation tie. Not flashy. Functional.
- **Shorts:** Khaki, regulation length. Ironed (somehow, in the woods).
- **Shoes:** Military-style boots. Laced tight. His one point of pride.
- **Posture:** ATTENTION. Straight back, chin up, chest out. He stands like he's being inspected. But there's a slight lean toward Brent -- he's always orbiting Brent's approval.
- **Merit badges:** Several, displayed prominently. He EARNS them. His dad checks.
- **Special detail:** A slightly too-tight belt. You know why.

**Idle animation:** Stands at attention, then occasionally breaks into a "ready" stance (slight crouch, hands in fists). Like he's waiting for an order. When Brent speaks, Josh nods -- a 1px head bob.

### NOAH

**From the script:** 12 and a half. The brains. Smart ass. 1400 PSAT in 7th grade. No bitches. His mom is a cunt (his words). He called his mom a cunt because she took his XBOX. He challenges Brent with logic.

**Sprite design (48x64):**
- **Hair:** Dark, slightly messy (not as neat as Sam, not as wild as Simon). Medium length, falls across the forehead. Almost emo-adjacent.
- **Build:** Average/lean. 14px shoulders, 58px tall. He doesn't care about his body -- he cares about his brain.
- **Scout shirt:** Worn CASUALLY. Unbuttoned over a dark t-shirt. Sleeves not rolled, just hanging. The shirt is almost a jacket, not a uniform. He's here because his mom made him be.
- **Neckerchief:** Pushed to the side or barely there. Maybe tied around his wrist instead of his neck. He's repurposed it.
- **Shorts:** Dark, non-regulation. He's wearing what he wants underneath.
- **Shoes:** Sneakers. Dark colored. Practical.
- **Posture:** Slightly slouched, one hand in pocket or crossed arms. His default is skeptical. His weight is back on his heels -- he's always ready to argue, not to comply.
- **Merit badges:** Has them but you can't see them because his shirt is open. They're on the inner part of the flap. He earned them but doesn't display them.
- **Special detail:** Glasses or no glasses? The script doesn't specify, but "the brains" kid with the PSAT score... give him glasses. Different from Brent's -- smaller, rounder. Smart-kid glasses vs. Brent's wider frames. This creates a visual parallel between the smartest kid and the kid who THINKS he's smart.

**Idle animation:** Arms crossed, weight shifting back. Occasionally pushes glasses up (1px hand-to-face movement). When someone says something stupid, a small head shake.

### LUCAS

**From the script:** 12. The quirky one. Pick me boy. Obsessed with dinosaurs and bugs. Meme legend. Super nice. Doesn't talk much but when he does it's about Spider-Man or serving women. His therapist says he's writing his ending before he's begun.

**Sprite design (48x64):**
- **Hair:** Messy, medium brown, sticking up in places. The kind of hair that's never been combed properly. Maybe a cowlick. 3-4 pixels of hair going in different directions at the top.
- **Build:** Thin. The thinnest. 12px shoulders, 56px tall. He takes up the least space. He's quiet and small and easily overlooked.
- **Scout shirt:** Wearing it but it's wrinkled. A button is in the wrong hole so it sits slightly crooked. There's a stain somewhere (bug-related, probably). He tried to iron it but gave up.
- **Neckerchief:** Actually kind of cute on him. He wears it like a fashion accessory. Maybe he tied it slightly differently -- in a bow instead of a knot? A small visual quirk.
- **Shorts:** Longer cargo shorts with visible POCKETS that look full. He collects things -- rocks, bugs, interesting sticks. His pockets bulge.
- **Shoes:** Red sneakers. Like Spider-Man's colors. This is his one expression of personality at his feet. Red Converse or similar. 2-3 pixels of bright red.
- **Posture:** Slightly hunched, looking around. His head is slightly tilted, like he's examining something. He's the kid who'd be looking at a bug while Brent is ranting about alpha males.
- **Merit badges:** The most UNUSUAL ones. Not shooting or camping -- nature study, insect study, reptile study. Weird patches. Maybe a Spider-Man pin (non-regulation, his secret rebellion).
- **Special detail:** Something in his hand. A stick, a rock, a bug. His idle sprite has him holding or examining a small object. He's always fiddling.

**Idle animation:** Fidgeting. Looking around. His head turns more than anyone else's -- he's observing the environment, not the social dynamics. Occasionally looks down at something in his hands.

## Environment Design (Informed by Production)

### Campfire Clearing

Based on the set: NOT a generic forest clearing. Specific elements:

- **The Branch Arch:** An arch of dead branches/sticks upstage center. This is the "entrance" to the clearing and it frames everything that happens. It looks like a ribcage or a cage. In the game, this should be a prominent background element -- dark wood against darker sky.
- **The Tire:** Center-stage, on the ground. Inside it: yellow-green ribbons/fabric representing the campfire that was NEVER LIT. This is Brent's broken promise of s'mores. The campfire is fake. Everything Brent promises is fake.
- **The Cooler:** White with blue lid. Stage right, near Brent's position. It glows slightly in the light. It contains horrors.
- **Wooden crates/blocks:** Scattered. Some stacked, some flat. Used as seats.
- **Rope:** Hanging from the arch, swaying slightly.
- **Ground:** Dirt, leaves, uneven. Not clean. Not safe.
- **Trees:** Dark trunks on the edges, canopy blocking most of the sky.
- **Stars:** Visible through gaps in the canopy. Small, cold, far away.
- **No campfire glow.** The clearing is lit by moonlight only. Cold.

**Lighting states for the campfire clearing:**
- **Opening/Oath:** Cold neutral. Blue-ish. Formal.
- **Sacrifice/Kiss test:** Getting warmer, more uncomfortable. Amber creeping in.
- **Pickup lines/Resistance:** Warmer. Red starting to appear at the edges.
- **Ceremony:** RED. The entire scene tints red as Brent escalates. The alpha chants should make the screen pulse red.
- **The Fight:** DEEP RED. Aggressive. The branch arch is backlit in red, making it look like fire or blood vessels.

### The Cliff (Gay Shit Act 1)

- **Open sky.** No canopy. This is the first time in the game the player sees open sky. That openness IS the relief.
- **Stars are BRIGHT and CLOSE.** Not the cold distant stars of the campfire. These stars are warm, golden-white, abundant.
- **Rock ledge** in the foreground. Flat, wide enough for two people.
- **Valley below** with tiny warm lights (a town, far away -- other people exist).
- **Wind effect:** Grass or hair moving slightly.
- **Purple/blue tinting.** Everything is washed in the blue-purple of the production photos. Soft. Safe.
- **No trees, no arch, no cooler.** All the symbols of Brent's world are GONE.

### The Meadow (Gay Shit Act 2)

- **Flowers.** Pink, blue, yellow, scattered across a gentle field.
- **Golden-hour lighting.** Warm. The warmest scene in the game. Orange/gold tint.
- **Low horizon.** Sky takes up more of the frame than ground.
- **Soft ground.** Grass, not dirt.
- **Butterflies?** Maybe. Simon likes butterflies. A 4x4 pixel butterfly moving across the background. Subtle.
- **Deeper purple/blue tint than the cliff but warmer.** The intimacy is deepening.

### The Lake (Gay Shit Act 3)

- **Still water** reflecting stars. The reflection doubles the starfield.
- **A flat rock** in the lower center. Intimate. Small. Two people fit, barely.
- **Surrounding darkness.** Trees at the very edges, but mostly it's water and sky.
- **Deep blue.** The deepest blue. Almost black but not quite. The most intimate color.
- **Moonlight on the water.** Silver-white. Beautiful.
- **This is the quietest visual in the game.** Minimal detail. Maximum feeling.

### The Void (Monologues)

Based on the monologue photos: **Single spotlight in absolute darkness.**

- **Black.** Not dark gray. Not dark blue. BLACK. #000000.
- **The spotlight** is a warm circle, not pure white. Slightly amber/gold. It feels like being seen -- which is terrifying and liberating.
- **The character stands in the center of the light.** Their shadow is visible on the ground.
- **The light pool is small.** The character barely fits. They're contained by it.
- **During Brent's final monologue:** The spotlight is RED. Not warm gold. Red. Blood red. And it's BIGGER than the others -- Brent takes up more space even when he's confessing.

## Sprite Animation System

### Movement Types

**Idle (all characters):**
Not just breathing. Each character has a DISTINCT idle that reflects who they are:
- Simon: Weight shift side-to-side, self-touch (arm hold), occasional look down
- Sam: Still, composed, slight forward lean, occasional side-glance toward Simon
- Brent: PACING (2-3px left-right), fist-into-palm, head surveillance turns
- Josh: At-attention stance, slight nod when Brent speaks, "ready" crouch
- Noah: Crossed arms, weight back, head shake, glasses push
- Lucas: Fidgeting, looking around, examining something in hands, head tilt

**Reaction animations (triggered by dialogue beats):**
- **Flinch:** Quick 2px backward jump + crouch (when Brent threatens)
- **Step forward:** 4px forward movement (when volunteering or being called out)
- **Step back:** 4px backward movement (when retreating)
- **Turn away:** Sprite flips horizontally or shifts to show back-turned
- **Slap:** Quick arm extension forward, 2 frames
- **Fall down:** Sprite drops to ground-level, becomes horizontal (Brent after the beating)
- **Kiss:** Two sprites move 2px closer, brief overlap, return (the kiss moments)
- **Run in circle:** Sprites orbit a center point during the alpha chant (use tween path)

**Formation states (group positions):**
- **LINE:** All 5 boys in a row, arms up (scout oath). Brent separate, pacing in front.
- **CIRCLE:** Boys arranged in a circle (alpha chants, running).
- **HUDDLE:** Boys close together (pre-fight tension).
- **CHAOS:** Random positions, overlapping, some on ground (fight).
- **PAIRED:** Simon + Sam close, others scattered (Gay Shit transitions).

### Costume State Machine

Each character sprite has multiple STATES that can be swapped:

**Simon:**
1. `simon-scout` -- Full scout uniform (campfire scenes)
2. `simon-pink` -- Pink striped shirt, no uniform (Gay Shit scenes)
3. `simon-bloody` -- Post-fight, blood on hands/clothes (if player chose violence)

**Sam:**
1. `sam-scout` -- Full proper uniform (campfire scenes)
2. `sam-relaxed` -- Unbuttoned shirt, softer (Gay Shit scenes)

**Brent:**
1. `brent-scout` -- Full uniform + cooler (most scenes)
2. `brent-ceremony` -- Shirt partially open, more unhinged (ceremony)
3. `brent-tank` -- White tank top, blood-spattered (final monologue)

**Josh, Noah, Lucas:**
1. `{name}-scout` -- Standard uniform
2. `{name}-fight` -- Disheveled, shirt torn/open (fight scene)

## The Silhouette Test

At 48x64 pixels scaled 3x, every character must be identifiable by SILHOUETTE ALONE:

- **Simon:** Shortest + curliest hair + tote bag strap = unique
- **Sam:** Average height + neat dark hair + upright posture = clean
- **Brent:** TALLEST + WIDEST + buzzcut + cooler nearby = dominant
- **Josh:** Stocky/wide for height + military stance = rigid
- **Noah:** Average + slouch + crossed arms + glasses = skeptical
- **Lucas:** Thin + messy hair + fidgeting hands + red shoes = quirky

If you squint and can still tell who is who, the design works.
